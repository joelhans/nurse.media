Who, or what, is Nurse?

It's a miracle anything lives here.

High elevation, 280-something days of intense sun every year, 110°F summers, an average of 12 inches of rain every year. And yet, the legendary saguaro cactus, the universal symbol for all deserts (it's even an emoji! 🌵) only grows in the Sonoran Desert in Arizona, California, and Mexico.

How, you might ask? Through chance, and a bit of help.

As a vulnerable seedling, the young saguaro has next to no chance of surviving the desert's extreme conditions. It needs the help of a mature mesquite, ironwood, or palo verde tree, otherwise known as a nurse tree. Over the next few decades (unlike your business, saguaros tend to be slow-growers), the cactus benefits from the nurse tree's microclimate of shade and humidity.

That relationship is the foundation of Nurse, and why I do want I do.
